Apalachee High School football team returns to home field for first time since shooting

By Blake DeVine
Published: Oct. 4, 2024 at 6:50 PM EDT

WINDER, Ga. (Atlanta News First) — It’s been one month since the mass shooting at Apalachee High School tragically took the lives of two teachers and two students.

On Friday night, the Wildcats hosted their first home football game since the shooting.

It was one of the first times that the football stadium was used since a candlelight vigil was held there to honor the four lives lost.

Andy Serrato is a senior at Apalachee High, who says this evening was emotional.

“One month ago, we were here on this football field, right after the incident,” he said. “It’s very important to show that we are able to get back up again.”

One of the four victims who lost their lives during the school shooting was Richard “Ricky” Aspinwall.

“Coach A” was on the hearts and minds of folks at the game tonight.

“It was very difficult for all of us, including students, teachers, faculty and the police,” Serrato said. “For anyone involved within that incident, this game right here is a symbol of strength.”

Courtney Dove’s daughter Cheyanne is a freshman at Apalachee High, who saw the shooting unfold on campus.

“She saw the shooter with the gun in his hand and he was running towards her,” Dove said. “Luckily she got into the classroom in time.”

She says her daughter is still struggling with what she witnessed.

Dove added that she had a hard time sending Cheyanne back to campus.

“I didn’t know if I was going to come to the game tonight, but you know Dwayne Johnson is here, so I wanted to see “The Rock”.

The Hollywood superstar was standing on the sideline, supporting the Wildcats throughout much of the game.

Last month, the team was able to meet Johnson at a production soundstage in Atlanta.

On Instagram, the Rock promised his support to the school, saying one of the best ways to heal “is through our smiles, laughter and joy.”

Jaelah Rodriguez is a junior at Apalachee High, who says the game helped her heal.

“Today is the day that we can all reclaim our school because it’s been a month today since it happened,” she concluded. “Everybody gets to spend time together and just realize we’re all here together for each other.”

Apalachee High ultimately fell to Jackson County by a final score of 25-6.

Head coach Mike Hancock said the community “[heals] through sports.”
