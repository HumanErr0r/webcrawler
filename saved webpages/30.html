How you can help Apalachee High School shooting victims and survivors

By Atlanta News First staff
Published: Sep. 5, 2024 at 11:37 AM EDT|Updated: Sep. 6, 2024 at 10:01 AM EDT

ATLANTA, Ga. (Atlanta News First) - As the community and country reel in the wake of a deadly shooting at Barrow County’s Apalachee High School, some are banding together to raise money for those affected.

GoFundMe has created a page for vetted fundraisers benefitting the shooting’s victims and survivors. The page will be updated as more fundraising efforts are verified.

Autoplay

One of the fundraisers is from Victims First, a California-based organization started by mass shooting survivors that raises money for shooting victims across the country. It is asking for $200,000, which will go directly to those impacted by the Apalachee High School shooting.

“Our families of previous mass shootings have been re-victimized when nonprofits say they are collecting donations for victims but do not actually give donations directly to victims,” Victims First wrote in a statement. “We, VictimsFirst, have started this fund so the victims’ families and those wounded are protected from fraud and exploitation.”

Fundraisers have also been verified for three of the dead: 14-year-old students Christian Angulo and Mason Schermerhorn’, and Ricky Aspinwall, an Apalachee High School math teacher.

The Barrow County Community Foundation is also raising money for those impacted.

Besides fundraising, community members and organizations are showing support in other ways.

Toni New is a chaplain with the Billy Graham Rapid Response Team, a non-profit that works to provide spiritual and emotional support in times of crisis.

“You don’t see a lot of tears, because they are in that stage of grief,” she said. “They’re in shock and some in denial that it happened.”

She said their team traveled to Barrow County to help the community heal from tragedy.

“We need healing, we need to be able to move forward and not be stuck. We hope to get them on that walk where they can go forward, not be stuck in what happened,” she said.

Meanwhile, Timothy Cullton with All County Towing has his team of drivers towing and dropping off cars for the school’s staff, students and families for free. He says those they have helped couldn’t bear returning to the school,

“My first thought is, ‘How can I help?’” said Cullton. He added that the families shouldn’t have to “worry about something so stressful as having to come to get their vehicle and have the rollercoaster of emotions coming back to this place in such a short amount of time.”

On Wednesday, four people died and nine were taken to the hospital with injuries after the shooting, according to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation. Colt Gray, a 14-year-old student, has been arrested and faces several murder charges.

Gray’s father, Colin Gray, was also taken into custody. Authorities believe the man gifted his son the gun that the teen allegedly used to carry out the shooting.
